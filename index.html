We stand with Ukraine

At the heart of the Ukraine - Russia crisis lies a fundamental question about the nature of history and nature of humanity: is change possible? Can we change the way we behave, or does history repeat itself endlessly, with humans forever condemned to re-enact past tragedies without changing anything except the decor? Is the world a jungle and should the strong prey upon the weak? Should the only thing preventing one country from attacking another be its military force?

We, the people who support sovereign independent Ukraine, believe that diplomacy and peaceful resolution of this conflict should be the only way forward - STOP THE WAR! We firmly oppose Russia’s threatening military aggression against Ukraine and it’s illegal annexation of Crimea. We oppose Russia’s recognition of Donbass regions of Ukraine’s sovereign territory as “independent”. Russia’s unprovoked and unjustified aggression by it’s military forces, the largest deployment on the European continent since the end of the Cold War, is a challenge to global security and the international order.

We reaffirm our solidarity with the people of Ukraine and our support to Ukraine’s efforts to strengthen its democracy and institutions, encouraging its defense. We consider it of utmost importance to help preserve the economic and financial stability of Ukraine and the safety and well-being of its people.

Russia must de-escalate and immediately withdraw its military forces from Ukraine.

Russia must de-escalate and immediately withdraw its military forces from Ukraine.

Ukraine has the right to exist as an independent nation and as a full member of the global community. Ukraine is and will be supported by the civilized world, billions of people who will respond with unity, clarity, and conviction to restrain the aggressor.

If you Stand With Ukraine, please, help spread this message globally by posting the statement icons and banners on your sites and social media profiles and linking back to this page. You can support Ukraine by making charitable contributions and donating to organizations such as “Come Back Alive” (#section tag to below) that have been actively operating in Ukraine since the outbreak of Ukraine - Russia conflict in 2014.
